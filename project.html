What is Nepalazole?

"Nepalazole" appears to be a misspelling or phonetic variation of Pantoprazole (often branded as Panzol, Panpazole, or Panzole), which is a common medication used in medicine to treat conditions caused by excessive stomach acid. It belongs to a class of drugs known as Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs).

what is Pulse oximeter

A pulse oximeter is a small, non-invasive device—usually clipped to the fingertip—that measures the percentage of oxygen saturation in the blood ( ) and heart rate. By emitting light through the skin, it detects how well oxygen is being delivered to the body's extremities, serving as a vital tool for monitoring conditions like COVID-19, COPD, and asthma

What is ECG?

An electrocardiogram (ECG or EKG) is a non-invasive, painless test that records the electrical signals of the heart to check for different heart conditions. It measures the timing and strength of electrical impulses, helping doctors detect arrhythmias, heart attacks, and structural issues

what is OT room.

An OT (Operating Theatre) room is a specialized, sterile hospital unit designed for performing surgical procedures, ranging from minor to major lifesaving operations. It is a highly controlled, sanitary environment containing specialized equipment, such as operating tables and imaging devices, designed to prevent infections.

what is Glucose meter.

A glucose meter is a small, portable device used to measure blood sugar levels in people with diabetes. It works by analyzing a small drop of blood from a finger stick, providing quick and accurate readings to help manage the condition.

What is fracture?

A fracture is a partial or complete break, crack, or split in a bone or hard material, usually caused by high-force impact, stress, or a twist. It is a medical term for a broken bone. Common examples include a fracture of the skull, leg, or arm, which are often treated with casts, splints, or surgery.
